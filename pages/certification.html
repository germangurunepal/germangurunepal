Certification Exams

German language proficiency can be certified through various exams. Here are some of the most recognized certification exams:

TestDaF

The TestDaF (Test Deutsch als Fremdsprache) is an internationally recognized language test for German as a foreign language. It is primarily aimed at those who want to study at a German university or need to provide evidence of their language skills for academic purposes. The test is available worldwide and evaluates reading, listening, writing, and speaking skills.

Learn more about TestDaF

Goethe-Zertifikat

The Goethe-Zertifikat is a series of German language exams offered by the Goethe-Institut, ranging from A1 to C2 levels. These exams are designed to measure proficiency according to the Common European Framework of Reference for Languages (CEFR). The certificates are widely recognized by employers and educational institutions.

Learn more about Goethe-Zertifikat

TELC

TELC (The European Language Certificates) offers a variety of German language exams for different proficiency levels and professional contexts. These exams are recognized by many institutions and employers in Europe and beyond.

Learn more about TELC

DSH

The DSH (Deutsche Sprachprüfung für den Hochschulzugang) is a German language proficiency test required for non-EU international students who wish to study at German universities. The exam assesses reading, listening, and writing skills, as well as the ability to understand and produce scientific language.

Learn more about DSH
